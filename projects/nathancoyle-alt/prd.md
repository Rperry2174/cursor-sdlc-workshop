# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

---

## Project Overview

**Project Name:** NBA Trade Machine

**One-line Description:** A single-page web app that lets you build hypothetical NBA trades between two teams and validates whether the trade is legal under the core CBA salary-matching rules.

**Type:** Web App (single HTML file with vanilla JS — no framework, no API, no database)

---

## Constraints (Workshop Rules)

- MVP must be buildable in ~10 minutes
- No database, no authentication, no external API calls at runtime
- Single page only — no routing
- All roster/salary data is **hardcoded in a JS data file** (a snapshot of current rosters)
- CBA rules are simplified for the MVP; more rules layered in as features

---

## CBA Rules We Care About (Simplified)

_Reference: 2023 NBA Collective Bargaining Agreement_

### MVP rule — Base salary matching (Article VII, Section 8)

For teams **over the salary cap**, outgoing salary in a trade must roughly match incoming salary:

| Outgoing Salary        | Maximum Incoming Salary          |
|------------------------|----------------------------------|
| $0 – $7.5M            | Outgoing + $5M                   |
| $7.5M – $29M          | Outgoing × 125% + $100K         |
| $29M+                  | Outgoing + $5M                   |

Teams **under the salary cap** can absorb players into their cap space without matching dollar-for-dollar (up to the cap).

### Post-MVP rules (features)

- **First Apron / Second Apron restrictions** — teams above the luxury tax aprons face additional constraints on what they can acquire
- **Trade exceptions (TPEs)** — a team can use a previously generated traded player exception to absorb salary without sending a player back
- **Simultaneous trades** — combining multiple trades to make salary math work

---

## Base MVP

**What the MVP includes:**
- A hardcoded dataset of **all 30 NBA teams** with their current rosters (player name + current-year salary), sourced from Basketball Reference
- Two side-by-side team selectors (dropdown to pick Team A and Team B)
- Each team's roster displayed as a checklist — check players to include in the trade
- A live "Trade Summary" panel showing:
  - Players and salaries going each direction
  - Total outgoing vs. incoming salary for each team
  - A **green check / red X** indicating whether the trade is legal under the base salary-matching rule above
- Clean, modern dark-themed UI styled to feel like an ESPN/NBA tool

**What it does NOT include (stretch goals):**
- Luxury tax / apron validation
- Trade exceptions or draft pick inclusion
- Multi-team (3+) trades
- Player stats or contract year details beyond current salary

## Features

### Feature 1: Luxury Tax & Apron Indicators
- **Description:** After the base trade legality check, show each team's projected luxury tax bill before and after the trade. Flag if a team crosses the First Apron ($178.1M) or Second Apron ($188.9M) and display a warning about the restrictions that apply (e.g., second-apron teams cannot acquire players via sign-and-trade, cannot use the taxpayer mid-level exception, etc.).
- **Files to create:** `base_mvp/tax-calculator.js` (luxury tax math + apron thresholds), update `index.html` to add a tax summary section below the trade result

### Feature 2: Trade Exceptions (TPEs)
- **Description:** Allow a team to use a Traded Player Exception instead of sending a player back. Add a "Use Trade Exception" toggle on each team's side. When enabled, the user can input a TPE dollar amount that counts as outgoing salary for matching purposes. Show the resulting TPE generated by the other team after the trade completes.
- **Files to create:** `base_mvp/trade-exceptions.js` (TPE logic — generation, usage, expiration display), update `index.html` to add TPE controls to each team panel

### Feature 3: Draft Pick Sweeteners
- **Description:** Let users attach draft picks (first-round or second-round, with year) to either side of the trade. Draft picks don't affect salary matching but are displayed in the trade summary for completeness. Include the Stepien Rule check: a team cannot trade first-round picks in consecutive years (must have at least one first-rounder in every other year).
- **Files to create:** `base_mvp/draft-picks.js` (pick data per team, Stepien Rule validation), update `index.html` to add a draft pick selector to each team panel
